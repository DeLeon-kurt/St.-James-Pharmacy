Isopropyl Alcohol

Isopropyl alcohol, also known as rubbing alcohol, is a common disinfectant used to clean and sanitize skin, surfaces, and medical instruments. It kills bacteria and viruses by denaturing their proteins and disrupting their cell membranes. To use isopropyl alcohol, apply it directly to the skin or surface using a cotton ball or pad. Allow it to air dry. Avoid contact with eyes, open wounds, or mucous membranes. Isopropyl alcohol should be used in a well-ventilated area and kept away from heat and flames due to its flammable nature.
